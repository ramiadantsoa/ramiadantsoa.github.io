Theory at the service of conservation


About

I am a Malagasy ecologist and develop mathematical and statistical models to better understand patterns and processes of biodiversity loss. I obtained a MSc in applied mathematics with Arthur Randrianarivony at the University of Antananarivo. I did a PhD with Ilkka Hanksi and Otso Ovaskainen at the University of Helsinki. I completed a first postdoc with Emma Goldberg at the University of Minnesota, and a second postdoc with Anthony Ives and Monica G. Turner at the University of Wisconsin-Madison (ACES), and is doing a third postdoc with Benjamin Roche at the University of Montpellier on COVID-19 modeling. I am the Scientific Director of Madagascar Biodiversity Center since 2023, and a Honorary Fellow of the University of Wisconsin-Madison since 2019.


Research

I work at the interface of theoretical ecology and conservation biology. Fill the gap in the research topic in Madagascar. I assess the role of environmental variability, spatial processes, and species interactions to understand stability and resilience of ecosystems. My work spans various continents: Asia, Europe, Northern America, and Madagascar, and involves topics such as phylogeography, paleoecology, and contemporary ecology. My research are generally motivated by conservation related questions in Madagascar.

Classical theoretical approach

Generate new theories on resilience, coexistence, and niche theory and effects of Fragmentation and habitat destruction (gen-spe, corridor, grassland), Stochastic process (shaky bowl, lottery, gen-spe), Environmental variability (Mada grassland).

Stat development

Developing and applying statistical methods. Phylogenetic comparative methods (GR model), Spore counting, and numerous topics

Bid data

With the help of data, review on regeneration, review on crop yield, review on time-series, review on phylogenetic patterns, and review on life-history traits.

    Teaching and outreach

    I spent a bit of my time showing how mathematics can be applied to ecology and conservation, and giving a glimpse of the life of a research to high school students in Madagascar. I also co-founded (with Cara Brook) and teach workshops (E2M2)to improve quantitative skills in Madagascar. Benjamin Rice and I are supporting (financially and academically) master's students in the STEM field until completion of the thesis.

    My other activites include fostering cultural exchanges between Madagascar and other countries. I am a member of two societies: the Finnish-based (Suomi-Madagascar Seura) and the Malagasy-based (Madagascar-Finland). On top of cultural exchanges, we organize fund raising to help underprivileged students in Madagascar.
