I am a theoretical ecologist with a main interest of understanding how environmental change in space or time and their interactions affect population and community structure. My main motivation is driven by conservation issuesâ€”elevated extinction risks for many fauna and flora. I address those questions through the lens of a: physiologist, ecologist, and evolutionary biologist.

I have a background in pure Mathematics in Madagascar, I did my PhD with Ilkka Hanksi and Otso Ovaskainen at the University of Helsinki, then a postdoc with Emma Goldberg at the University of Minnesota, and currently I am one of the four postdocs at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in the project: Abrupt Changes in Ecological systems lead by Monica Turner, Anthony Ives, Steve Carpenter, Jack Williams, and Chris Kucharick.
